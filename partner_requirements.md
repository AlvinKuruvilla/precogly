# Partner Requirements

## Background

Precogly is an open-source threat modeling platform. As adoption grows, enterprises often look for practitioners who can help them deploy, customize, and operationalize the tool in real-world environments.

This partner listing (./partners.md) exists to make it easier for end-user teams and enterprises to discover individuals and organizations who have demonstrated practical engagement with Precogly.

This is not a formal commercial program. It is an early ecosystem initiative intended to connect users with experienced contributors and implementers.

## Disclaimer

Listing in the Precogly partner directory in ./partners.md does not constitute endorsement, certification, or guarantee of any partner's services, capabilities, or quality of work. Precogly does not verify partner engagements with end users, and all service arrangements are solely between the partner and the client.

## What Partners Get

- Visibility on the Precogly GitHub repository (`partners.md`)
- Potential visibility on https://precogly.org/partners
- Early association with the Precogly ecosystem as it evolves
- The ability to shape the platform through real-world usage and feedback
- Partners may describe themselves as a "Precogly Listed Partner" or "Precogly Founding Partner" if amongst the first 10 partners.

## Founding Partners

The first set of partners who meet the requirements will be recognized as Founding Partners.

- Limited to the first 10 qualifying partners
- May receive additional visibility in early Precogly materials

This designation reflects early involvement in shaping the ecosystem.

## Partner Specializations

Each partner listing includes one or more specializations so that enterprises can find help relevant to their needs. Examples include:

- **Integration** (e.g., Jira, ServiceNow, CI/CD pipelines)
- **AI Agent Development** (e.g., building threat modeling assistants on the Precogly API)
- **Training and Workshops** (e.g., onboarding teams, threat modeling methodology training)
- **Deployment and Operations** (e.g., self-hosted setup, SSO configuration, migration)
- **Consulting** (e.g., threat model reviews, program design, compliance mapping)

Partners select their specializations when applying. The Precogly maintainers may adjust these based on demonstrated experience.

## Requirements

To be listed as a partner both the following requirements must be met:

1. Raise at least 5 non-trivial issues on the Precogly GitHub repository. Issues must be accepted or acknowledged by a maintainer. Examples of qualifying issues: bug reports with reproduction steps, well-defined enhancement proposals, or substantive UX feedback.
2. Create at least 1 public resource (blog, video, tutorial, demo, or deployment writeup) that demonstrates hands-on use of Precogly.

## Staying Listed

Partners are expected to maintain a minimum level of ongoing engagement:

- At least 1 meaningful contribution (issue, PR, or public resource) per year
- Partners who have not contributed in the past 12 months will be contacted before removal

This ensures the listing reflects active participants, not one-time contributors.

## Process

- Submit your public resource via a PR to `resources.md`
- Share your GitHub issue contributions via a GitHub issue
- Include your preferred specialization(s) in your application
- The Precogly project core maintainer(s) will review for relevance and completeness
- Qualified entries will be added to `partners.md` and `resources.md`

## After the Founding Partners

Once the 10 founding partner slots are filled, the program continues under the same requirements. New partners are reviewed on a rolling basis. The maintainers may adjust requirements over time as the ecosystem matures.

## Removal

This list is curated by the Precogly project. Entries may be updated or removed at any time, including in cases of:

- Misrepresentation of capabilities or association with Precogly
- Dishonest or unethical conduct
- Inactivity or stale engagement (see "Staying Listed" above)

Where possible, communication will be attempted before removal.
